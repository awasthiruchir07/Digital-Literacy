#  UPI / Online Payment Fraud – Case Study

## Overview

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into authorizing unauthorized transactions. It mainly relies on social engineering rather than technical hacking.

---

##  How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. The victim receives a call, SMS, or message posing as a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support  
2. The fraudster creates urgency (refund, reward, or account issue)  
3. The victim is asked to click a link, install an app, or approve a **collect request**  
4. Sometimes, the victim is asked to share OTP or UPI PIN  
5. Once approved, money is instantly transferred to the fraudster’s account  

---

##  Who is Targeted?

- College students and young users  
- People new to digital payments  
- Users who are unaware of cyber fraud techniques  

---

##  Consequences

- Immediate financial loss  
- Mental stress and panic  
- Difficulty in recovering money if not reported quickly  

---

##  Key Takeaway

- Never share your **UPI PIN or OTP**  
- Always verify payment requests before approving  
- Avoid clicking on unknown links
